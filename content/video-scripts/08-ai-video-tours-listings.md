## Main Content (0:30-9:30)

### Why Video Tours Are Non-Negotiable (0:30-1:30)
The data is clear. Listings with video get significantly more engagement. But here's what most agents miss — it's not about production quality. It's about speed and consistency.

Buyers want to see a home before committing to a showing. If you don't have video, you're losing half your potential interest. If you have video but it's from two months ago, you're showing outdated information.

The solution: Create AI-assisted video tours in under 15 minutes that look professional and stay current.

### The 15-Minute Video System (1:30-4:00)

#### Step 1: Script with AI (3 minutes)
"Write a 90-second video script for a home tour of [ADDRESS]. 

Property features: [BEDS] bedrooms, [BATHS] bathrooms, [SPECIAL FEATURES]

Script should include:
- Opening hook (5 sec): Something that creates curiosity
- Front porch/entrance (10 sec): Set the scene
- Living areas (20 sec): Key features
- Kitchen (15 sec): Focal points
- Primary bedroom (15 sec): Stand-out elements
- Backyard/outdoor space (15 sec): Lifestyle angle
- Closing tour (10 sec): Invite to schedule showing

Tone: Enthusiastic but authentic, like a knowledgeable friend showing you around. Not a narrator."

#### Step 2: Record with Your Phone (5 minutes)
- Use natural light when possible
- Move slowly — this isn't action camera footage
- Hold phone horizontal (landscape mode)
- Get every room, even small ones
- Capture the "wow" moments — the view, the updated kitchen, the backyard

#### Step 3: Edit with AI (7 minutes)
For editing, I use tools like CapCut or the built-in tools in Canva. But AI is making this even easier:

"Edit this property tour video to:
- Cut out dead space and pauses
- Add music from [MOOD — upbeat, chill, luxury]
- Include text overlays for key features ([BEDS] bed, [LOCATION])
- Add a branded intro and outro with my contact info
- Keep total runtime under 2 minutes"

This is now possible with tools like Runway or similar AI video editors.

### The AI Enhancement Tools (4:00-6:00)

#### For Virtual Staging:
Empty rooms kill buyer interest. AI virtual staging shows potential:

"I have photos of an empty [ROOM TYPE] at [ADDRESS]. Generate 3 different furniture styles that would appeal to [TARGET BUYER — young family, retiree, investor]."

Tools like Virtual Staging AI or even Canva's AI can do this now.

#### For Background Removal/Enhancement:
Remove distracting elements, enhance the sky, make the grass look greener:

Use before/after comparisons in your listing description. "Virtual staging applied to show potential — actual photos available upon request."

#### For Script Generation:
Already covered. Use the ChatGPT prompt to create your tour script in under 3 minutes.

### Listing Video Must-Haves (6:00-7:30)

Every listing video should include:

#### 1. The Address in Text
Overlay the address prominently. People need to know which property this is.

#### 2. Key Stats
"Beds | Baths | Sq Ft | [NOTABLE FEATURE]"
Keep this visible for several seconds in each room.

#### 3. Neighborhood Context
Don't just show inside — include:
- Street view
- Nearby amenities
- School district badge
- Commute time highlights

#### 4. Your Contact Info
End card with name, phone, website. Make it easy to reach you.

#### 5. Call to Action
"Schedule a showing at [LINK]" or "Call/text [NUMBER]"

### The Drone Question (7:30-8:30)

Yes, aerial footage adds value. No, you don't need to buy a drone.

Options:
- Hire a local drone operator ($100-200 per listing)
- Use FPV drone services that specialize in real estate
- For budget: Use satellite imagery from Google Earth in your video editor

The aerial shot isn't mandatory, but it does make a difference in the final video quality perception.

### The YouTube Optimization (8:30-9:30)

If you're posting listing videos to YouTube (and you should be for MLS embeds), optimize:

Title: "[Address] — [Beds] Bed [City] Home Tour | [Community Name]"

Description: Include full property details, address, your contact info, and a link to schedule showing.

Tags: City, neighborhood, beds, baths, "[City] real estate", "[City] homes for sale"

Thumbnail: Use a frame from the video with the address and bed/bath count as text overlay. Make it pop.
