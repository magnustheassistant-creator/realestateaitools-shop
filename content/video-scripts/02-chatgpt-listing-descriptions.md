## Hook (0:00-0:30)
"Your listing description is your first impression with buyers online. Most agents write '3 bedroom, 2 bathroom home in great location.' No wonder they're invisible. Today I'm sharing the exact ChatGPT prompts that turn boring listings into buyer magnets — and you can copy them right now."

## Main Content (0:30-9:30)

### The Conversion Problem (0:30-1:30)
Here's the reality: buyers scroll through hundreds of listings. Yours needs to stop them. But most agents write descriptions like they're filling out a form. "3 bed, 2 bath, granite counters." That's not a story — that's inventory.

The difference between a listing that gets 5 views and one that gets 50 is emotional connection. Buyers aren't buying square footage — they're buying their future life in that home. Your description needs to paint that picture.

### The Framework (1:30-3:00)
Before I give you the prompts, you need to understand the framework. Every converting listing description has four elements:

One: Sensory language. Not "granite counters" but "countertops that feel cool beneath your fingertips as morning light streams through the window."

Two: Lifestyle hooks. What could the buyer DO in this home? Not just what rooms exist.

Three: Neighborhood context. Buyers need to imagine their daily life — commute, coffee shops, evening walks.

Four: Social proof and scarcity. "Walking distance to top-rated elementary school" or "One of only 3 homes in this community with a pool."

### The Prompts (3:00-8:00)

#### Prompt 1: The Emotional Transformation
Use this prompt when you have a great property but need to tell its story:

"Write a listing description for a [HOME TYPE] in [CITY/NEIGHBORHOOD]. The home features [KEY FEATURES]. My target buyer is [DESCRIPTION]. Write in second person, focusing on how this home transforms their daily life. Include sensory details — what they see, hear, smell, feel. Add one surprising detail that makes this home memorable. Keep it under 200 words but make every sentence count. End with a call to action that creates urgency."

This prompt works because it forces ChatGPT out of listing-speak and into transformation language.

#### Prompt 2: The Differentiation Prompt
Use this when you're in a competitive market and need to stand out:

"You are a real estate copywriter. Write a listing description that makes [ADDRESS] feel different from every other home on the market. The unique selling points are [LIST]. Target buyer is [DESCRIPTION]. Create intrigue in the first two sentences — don't lead with beds/baths. Write 150 words maximum. Include one line about what makes the neighborhood special. End with a low-pressure question that prompts engagement."

#### Prompt 3: The Luxury Upgrade
For higher-end listings:

"Write a luxury listing description for [ADDRESS]. This home is priced at $[PRICE] and features [AMENITIES]. Write it in elevated, editorial style. Use active voice. Include architectural details. Paint a scene of the ideal buyer's morning routine in this home. Create a sense of aspiration. Do not use: 'beautiful', 'amazing', 'stunning', 'perfect' — show these qualities through description instead. 200-250 words."

#### Prompt 4: The FSBO特别版
For sale by owners who need help:

"Write a listing description for a home being sold by owner at [ADDRESS]. Key features: [LIST]. The seller is motivated because [CONTEXT — don't reveal price]. Write it so it doesn't feel like a for-sale-by-owner listing. Include an invitation to contact the owner directly. Make it warm and personal. 175 words."

### Testing and Refinement (8:00-9:00)
Here's what most agents miss: they don't test variations. For the same listing, write three different descriptions using these prompts. Then A/B test them. Look at which gets more showings, faster sales, higher offers.

Also, always edit what ChatGPT gives you. The AI is your first draft partner, not your final copywriter. Add specific details only you know — the coffee shop the previous owner mentioned, the way the light hits the kitchen at 4pm, the neighbor who waves.

### The One Rule (9:00-9:30)
If your listing description could be copy-pasted to any other home in any other city, you've failed. Every description should feel like it could only belong to that specific property. Use ChatGPT to get started, but make it unmistakably yours.
