## Main Content (0:30-9:30)

### The Texting Problem (0:30-1:30)
Buyers expect responses in minutes, not hours. But you're busy showing homes, writing offers, living your life. So what happens? You forget to respond. Or you respond at 11pm and sound like you're desperate. Or you send a template that sounds like everyone else's template.

The problem isn't that you don't want to respond — it's that manual texting doesn't scale. You can't be available 24/7 and maintain quality.

But AI can. Here's how to set up text automation that feels human.

### The 5 Text Sequences to Automate (1:30-4:00)

#### Sequence 1: New Lead Response (The Golden Hour)
Template: "Hey [NAME]! Thanks for reaching out. I saw you're interested in [PROPERTY/AREA]. I'm actually with a client right now but wanted to connect. When's a good time to call — tomorrow afternoon or evening work for you?"

This goes out immediately (within 5 minutes of lead submission). It's warm, sets expectations, and moves toward a call.

#### Sequence 2: Property Match Alert
Template: "Hey [NAME], just saw a new listing that matches what you're looking for — [BEDS] beds, [AREA], under [PRICE]. It's a new match for you. Want me to set up a showing? Link: [CALENDLY]"

Trigger: When new listing comes on market matching lead criteria. This creates urgency and shows you're on top of things.

#### Sequence 3: Showing Follow-Up
Template: "Hey [NAME]! How'd it go at [PROPERTY] today? Would love to hear your thoughts. Any questions I can answer?"

Trigger: 2 hours after showing. Shows you care, not just making a sale.

#### Sequence 4: Re-Engagement for Cold Leads
Template: "Hey [NAME] — I know life gets busy. Just checking in — still thinking about making a move [TIMEFRAME]? If timing's shifted, no worries. But if you're ready to talk, I'm here."

Trigger: 14 days of no response. Low pressure, non-annoying.

#### Sequence 5: Market Update
Template: "Hey [NAME] — quick market update: [KEY INSIGHT]. Thought of you since [PERSONALIZED REASON]. Questions? Just reply here."

Trigger: Monthly for warm leads. Provides value, stays top of mind.

### The Personalization Framework (4:00-6:00)

AI makes this scalable. Here's the prompt:

"Create a text message for a real estate lead named [NAME]. 

Their situation: [DETAILS — buyer, seller, timeline, property type]
My relationship: [HOW WE MET — sphere, referral, online inquiry]

Message type: [NEW LEAD / PROPERTY MATCH / FOLLOW-UP / RE-ENGAGEMENT / MARKET UPDATE]

Requirements:
- Under 25 words
- Conversational, like texting a friend
- Include their name
- [CUSTOM ELEMENT — specific property, neighborhood, timeline]
- Low pressure CTA

Tone: Warm, helpful, not salesy."

This generates personalized texts in seconds. You can save the outputs and use them in your CRM automation.

### The Tools to Make This Work (6:00-7:30)

#### Follow Up Boss
Built-in text automation with personalization tokens. You can set up sequences with [FIRSTNAME], [PROPERTY], [ADDRESS], etc. It pulls from the CRM data.

#### Manychat
If you want more sophisticated automation — branching logic, keyword responses, AI-powered conversations — Manychat is the standard. Connect it to your phone number and build flows.

#### Simple text via Google Voice
For simpler needs, you can set up Google Voice with auto-reply rules. Not as sophisticated but works for basic coverage.

#### The Key Integration
Whatever tool you use, it needs to connect to your CRM. When a lead enters your system, they should immediately enter the text sequence. No manual triggers.

### The Response System (7:30-9:00)

When leads RESPOND to your automated texts, you need a plan:

#### Immediate (within 15 minutes)
Whatever comes in, acknowledge receipt. "Got it! Give me a few to look into this."

#### Within 2 hours
Actually answer their question or take the requested action.

#### The AI Assistant for Responses
For common questions, have template responses ready:

"Write 10 response templates for common real estate text questions:
- 'What's the process?'
- 'Can we see it tomorrow?'
- 'Is the price negotiable?'
- 'How long has it been on market?'
- 'Tell me about the neighborhood'

Format: Lead says [X]. You reply [Y]."

### The Line Between Automation and Annoying (9:00-9:30)

Rules I follow:
1. Never more than 3 texts in a sequence without a response
2. Always respect opt-out — if they say stop, stop
3. Add something unique in each text when possible — a detail about them, their search, their situation
4. If they call, get off auto-pilot and be fully present

The goal is enhanced presence, not replaced presence. AI handles the early touchpoints so you're free to handle the conversations that matter.
