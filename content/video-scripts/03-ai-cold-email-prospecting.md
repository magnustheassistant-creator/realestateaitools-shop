## Main Content (0:30-9:30)

### Why Most Cold Email Fails (0:30-1:30)
Let me tell you what I see agents do wrong constantly. They send the same generic "Hi, I'm a real estate agent in your area, let's connect" email to 500 people and wonder why nobody responds.

Here's the math that changes everything: In a typical farm area, you might have 10,000 households. If you send generic emails, you might get a 0.5% response rate — 50 people. But if you personalize and get a 3% response rate, that's 300 people. Same effort, 6x the results.

AI makes personalization at scale possible. Not fake "Hi [NAME]" personalization — real, research-based personalization that makes people feel seen.

### Template 1: The Curiosity Hook (1:30-3:00)
This is my highest-performing email for expired listing leads:

Subject: [FIRST NAME], question about your old kitchen

Body:
"I drove by [ADDRESS] last week — beautiful home. Question: when you lived there, was the kitchen the heart of the house?

I'm working with a buyer right now who reminds me of you — they want a home where their family actually spends time together, not just in separate rooms. They loved the neighborhood but said the kitchen felt disconnected from the living space.

I'd love to show them your former home. Would you have 15 minutes this week to chat about what's possible?

[YOUR NAME]"

Notice: I'm not leading with "I want to list your home." I'm creating curiosity and showing I've done my research. The question makes them think. The buyer story makes it relevant.

### Template 2: The Value-First Approach (3:00-4:30)
For SOI and sphere database:

Subject: [FIRST NAME], something for your portfolio

Body:
"[FIRST NAME], I came across something that might interest you.

[data point about their neighborhood — recent sale, price change, market shift]

I thought of you because [personalized reason — they're a landlord, they mentioned real estate before, they recently moved to the area, etc].

Not sure if the timing is right, but if you ever want a quick chat about what I'm seeing in the market, my calendar is open Thursday morning.

No pressure — just thought you should know.

[YOUR NAME]"

The key here is lead with value, not with your agenda. You're giving them market intelligence. You're thinking of them. That's memorable.

### Template 3: The Video Touch (4:30-5:30)
For high-value leads you really want:

Subject: [FIRST NAME] — quick video for you

Body:
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

I made a 60-second video about [RELEVANT TOPIC — their neighborhood, a property that matches their search, market conditions].

Watch it here: [LINK]

I'd love to hear your thoughts — and if the timing makes sense, I'd welcome 10 minutes on your calendar.

[YOUR NAME]"

Video emails stand out because almost nobody does them. It builds know, like, and trust faster than text.

### The AI Personalization Formula (5:30-7:30)
Here's the prompt I use to turn a basic template into a personalized email:

"You are a real estate agent in [CITY]. I need to reach out to [LEAD TYPE — expired owner, FSBO, buyer, sphere] named [NAME]. Here is what I know about them: [RELEVANT DETAILS — property address, timeline, search criteria, relationship to you].

Write a 150-word cold email that:
- Has a subject line that creates curiosity
- Opens with something that shows I've done research on their specific situation
- Includes one specific detail that makes this email feel personal
- Provides value, not just a sales pitch
- Has a low-pressure call to action
- Sounds like a real person, not a template

Do not use: 'I hope this finds you well' or 'I wanted to reach out' as opening lines."

This prompt works because it forces specific research and creates genuine personalization.

### Timing and Follow-Up (7:30-8:30)
The email is step one. Follow-up is where deals are made.

My sequence:
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 2: Follow-up text — "Hey [NAME], did you get a chance to see my email? Happy to jump on a call if timing is right."
- Day 5: Second email with new value — market update, new listing, relevant content
- Day 10: Break-up email — "I don't want to keep bugging you — if timing isn't right, totally understand. But if anything changes, I'm here."

The break-up email actually gets responses from people who've been ignoring you. It triggers the "oh, I should respond to that" reflex.

### The Subject Line Formula (8:30-9:30)
Your subject line is 70% of your success. Here's my formula:

[First Name] + Curiosity/Value/Question + Specific Detail

Examples:
- "Sarah, question about Oak Street"
- "Tom, congrats on the new place"
- "Sarah, something about the neighborhood"
- "Tom, your old street is getting active"

First name in the subject increases open rates by 20%. Specific detail increases relevance. Curiosity/value makes them actually read it.
