### The Transaction Pipeline (1:45-3:30)

Every transaction goes through these stages:

1. Lead → Pre-approval → Under Contract → Inspection → Appraisal → Financing Approval → Closing

Within each stage, there are:
- Documents needed
- Deadlines to track
- People to notify
- Tasks to complete

AI can track all of this and send reminders BEFORE something is due, not after.

### The AI Transaction System (3:30-5:30)

#### Document Organization
Every deal needs a folder structure:

- Contact Info
- Pre-approval Letter
- Purchase Agreement
- Inspection Reports
- Appraisal
- Financing Documents
- Title & Escrow
- Closing Documents

AI tools can auto-file emails and documents into the right folder based on content. But you need to set this up initially.

#### Deadline Tracking
Use a CRM or transaction management tool (TransactionDesk, Dotloop, or Follow Up Boss) to track every deadline. Set reminders:
- 7 days before
- 3 days before  
- 1 day before
- Day of

AI can actually send automated reminders to all parties — buyers, sellers, agents, lenders.

#### Task Automation
"Create a transaction checklist template for a standard residential sale in [STATE]. 

Include:
- All document deadlines
- Inspection requirements
- Financing milestones
- Closing prep tasks
- Communication touchpoints

For each task, note: Who is responsible? What triggers the task? What happens if it's missed?"

This becomes your master template for every transaction.

### The Follow-Up System (5:30-7:00)

Communication is where deals die or close. Here's my AI-assisted follow-up:

#### Week 1 After Contract:
Day 1: Send welcome packet with timeline expectations
Day 3: Check in — "Any questions about the inspection process?"
Day 7: Send market update relevant to their transaction

#### Week 2-4 (Inspection to Appraisal):
Automated updates when milestones are hit:
- Inspection complete → Document filed → Next steps sent
- Appraisal scheduled → Reminder to buyer
- Appraisal complete → Share results with context

#### Final Week:
Day -7: Confirm closing time and location
Day -3: Final walkthrough reminder
Day -1: "See you tomorrow!" message
Day of: "Excited to close with you!"

### The AI Communication Templates (7:00-8:30)

"Write transaction update templates for a real estate agent:

1. Welcome message (sent at contract)
2. Milestone update (inspection complete, appraisal scheduled, etc)
3. Challenge notification (if issues arise)
4. Closing reminder sequence (7 days, 3 days, 1 day, day of)
5. Post-closing thank you

Format: 
- Subject line
- Body (under 100 words each)
- Tone: Professional, reassuring, never panicked
- Include relevant contact information and next step instructions"

These go in your CRM and get sent automatically when you mark a milestone complete.

### The Emergency Protocol (8:30-9:30)

When something goes wrong — and it will — you need a system:

#### Step 1: Don't Panic
Your emotional state affects everyone around you. Take a breath.

#### Step 2: Assess Impact
How does this affect the timeline? The deal terms? The relationship?

#### Step 3: Communicate Proactively
Don't hide problems. Buyers and sellers respect honesty.

Template: "I need to let you know about [SITUATION]. Here's what happened and here's what I'm doing about it. I'll update you by [TIME] with [SPECIFIC UPDATE]."

#### Step 4: Solve or Escalate
Can you solve it? Do it. Do you need help? Call your broker or transaction coordinator.

The goal is to never have a problem become a surprise. The more proactive your communication, the more trust you build — even when things go wrong.
