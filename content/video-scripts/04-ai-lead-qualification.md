# Video Script 04: AI Lead Qualification — Stop Wasting Time on Dead Leads

## Hook (0:00-0:30)

## Main Content (0:30-9:30)

### The Qualification Problem (0:30-1:45)
I want you to track something for one week: how many hours do you spend on leads who never convert? Not just leads who don't answer — leads who answer, you meet with them, you work with them for months, and then nothing happens.

For most agents, it's shocking. 40% of their time goes to leads that were never truly qualified in the first place.

The old way: call everyone and hope. The AI way: score everyone and prioritize.

### The Lead Scoring Framework (1:45-4:00)

#### Demographic Signals
These tell you if they CAN buy/sell:
- Property ownership status
- Estimated home value
- Time in current residence
- Equity position
- Life stage indicators (new baby, retirement, divorce = motivated)

#### Behavioral Signals  
These tell you if they WILL buy/sell soon:
- Website activity — which properties viewed, how long
- Email engagement — opens, clicks, replies
- Search behavior — price range changes, location changes
- Outreach actions — submitted form, called, scheduled tour

#### Intent Signals
These tell you they're READY now:
- Asked about financing or mortgage options
- Requested specific showing times
- Inquired about closing timeline
- Compared multiple properties seriously

AI tools like Follow Up Boss combine all three signal types into a single lead score from 1-100. But the real power is understanding WHY they're scored that way.

### The AI Qualification Stack (4:00-6:00)

#### Tool 1: Follow Up Boss
Tracks all behavioral signals automatically. Every website visit, every email open, every click is logged. The AI scores leads based on engagement patterns from your entire database.

When I get a new lead, I can see their score and compare it to leads who've converted vs. gone cold. If someone comes in at 85+ and has high intent signals, they're calling within 24 hours.

#### Tool 2: ChatGPT for Qualification Calls
I use this for phone qualification conversations:

"Help me create a phone script for qualifying a real estate lead. I need to understand: are they motivated, are they qualified, and what's their timeline?

Create a conversational flow that:
- Opens with rapport-building
- Covers motivation without being pushy
- Asks about timeline naturally
- Qualifies budget and property needs
- Discovers the real decision-maker
- Ends with clear next step

Make it sound natural, not like an interrogation."

I copy this into my phone notes and reference it during calls. It helps me stay focused on qualification without missing important signals.

#### Tool 3: Veloteam or Similar for Text Analysis
This is emerging tech: AI that analyzes voice conversations and flags key moments. You upload a call recording, it transcribes it and highlights qualification gems: "mentioned they need to move by July," "said they're pre-approved," "revealed they're working with another agent."

### Your Week-One Action Plan (6:00-8:00)

#### Day 1: Clean Your Database
Export your current leads. Score them manually using the framework above. Separate into: hot (score 70+), warm (40-69), cold (below 40).

For cold leads, set up a long-term nurture sequence. For hot leads, call today.

#### Day 2: Implement Auto-Scoring
Connect your lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, website) to Follow Up Boss. Let the AI start tracking behaviors immediately.

#### Day 3-5: Create Qualification Sequences
Build different follow-up paths based on lead source and initial score. FSBO leads get different content than sphere leads. Website leads get different nurture than referral leads.

#### Day 6-7: Audit and Adjust
Review your scores. Are the leads that converted actually your highest-scored ones? If not, adjust your scoring criteria.

### The Qualification Questions That Matter (8:00-9:30)

Instead of "Are you working with an agent?" (which puts them on guard), try:

"If you found the perfect home tomorrow, what would need to happen for you to be able to make an offer?" — This reveals financing status, timeline pressure, and decisiveness.

"What's bringing you back to look at homes now versus a year ago?" — This surfaces motivation and urgency.

"If you don't mind me asking, what kind of timeline are you working with?" — Direct but still comfortable.

"Who else is involved in this decision?" — Critical for qualification because you might be talking to the wrong person.

Remember: your goal isn't to reject people — it's to prioritize your time for people who are ready and able to work with you.
